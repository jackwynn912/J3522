LeBron James Through the Years


To many, LeBron James is recognized as the greatest basketball player of all time. However, it's not only his skill that impresses fans, it's how he has been able to sustain a successful career for this long. Now in his 22nd season, LeBron continues to prove that his longevity is truly unprecedented. Despite his accomplishments on the court, he is equally admirable off it. LeBron has excelled as a father, helping his son achieve his dream of playing in the NBA, and as a philanthropist. LeBron has built schools through the "I PROMISE" foundation and has given out thousands of college scholarships. This page is built to recognize just how long LeBron has been a public figure and the significance of his achievements.

So what makes LeBron the greatest player of all time? For many fans, it's because of his resilience. LeBron faced many hardships at the start of his career. Growing up in Akron, LeBron viewed basketball as his way out, a chance to make a name for himself and for his mom. Despite the financial stability being in the NBA provided, from a basketball standpoint, the Cavaliers were as unstable as you could get. Despite dealing with bad ownership and underperforming teammates, LeBron still brought the Cavaliers to the finals and won two MVP awards. He fully established himself during his time with the Heat when he won two titles, two more MVPs and made the finals all four years he played there. In fact, between Miami, his second stint in Cleveland, and his time with the Lakers, LeBron brought every new team he joined a championship. When you combine these feats with his historic athleticism and knowledge of the game, you get the greatest player ever.
